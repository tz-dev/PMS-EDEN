1. Purpose and scope of this specification

This document specifies the PMS-EDEN layer in a concise, technical form. It is based on the YAML profile PMS-EDEN.yaml (with schema_version = "PMS-EDEN_1.2") and renders its structure, constraints, and semantics explicit for human readers and computational systems.

PMS-EDEN is an application profile (overlay) of the Praxeological Meta-Structure (PMS; Δ–Ψ). It does not redefine PMS operators, dependencies, layers, or derived axes. Instead, it formalizes an Eden drift lens: a structurally minimal grammar for how a praxis configuration can drift into comparison governance, pseudo-symmetry, asymmetry-switch availability, permanentized exception, responsibility displacement, residual stabilization, devaluation or humiliation, and reciprocity loss—read strictly as operator configurations (Δ–Ψ) under asymmetry (Ω), temporality (Θ), distance (Χ), integration (Σ), and self-binding (Ψ).

All PMS-EDEN labels remain paper-internal, descriptive, scene-bound, revisable, and non-totalizing. They are not PMS operators, not diagnoses, not ideology labels, not person categories, not essence claims, and not authority claims.

The specification covers, in particular:

  • the schema_meta block (identity, status, authorship, dependency on PMS_1.3, authoritative source-paper role);
  • the validity_gate enforcing Χ (Distance), reversibility, and D (Dignity-in-Practice) for PMS-valid application only;
  • paper-internal terms from §0.7: asymmetry switch, permanentized exception switch, parity-to-exit, and responsibility-to-authority;
  • paper constructs (NRK, PS, PFO, reciprocity, devaluation, humiliation, adult reciprocity as non-externalization) as non-operators;
  • reduced_signatures and the drift_catalogue, including the updated master trace with explicit switch drift and residual stabilization;
  • viability as paper-bound cost/coordination readout under PMS criteria as used in this paper;
  • EDEN-MAP as scene-bound mapping protocol with Q1–Q7, including switch-drift scan and diagnostics-to-action firewall;
  • pattern_library with pattern-status clarification and paper-internal composite discipline;
  • glossary handles (stable refs; canonical text remains in the paper);
  • alternative explanations (methodological discipline box; sufficiency, no ranking, including AE4 external institutional stress);
  • boundary conditions (scope discipline, rival-framework openness, genuine non-capture, strongest-applicability summary);
  • appendix positioning (adjacent frameworks, non-goals, classification risk controls, theory-adjacent stance);
  • example-suite schema (repo vignettes; scene-bound, non-instructional, dignity-preserving, switch-drift-aware).
Core idea drift as operator-carriage

PMS-EDEN treats “Eden” as a minimal structural testbed: a praxis-first scene where drift becomes legible by tracking what operators carry, fail to carry, displace, or simulate. The overlay’s job is to keep the analysis structural, scene-bound, auditable, revisable, rival-framing-open, and dignity-preserving while making switch drift, cost geometry, and reciprocity loss inspectable.

Conceptual separation PMS vs PMS-EDEN

PMS defines the operator grammar (Δ–Ψ) and derived axes (A, C, R, E, D). PMS-EDEN defines a drift-reading discipline: Eden sequences, frames, non-events, regimes, switch availability, costs, reciprocity loss, and mapping protocol—without introducing new operators, new axioms, or person-level explanations.

3. Validity gate and scope constraints

3.1 PMS-valid application gate

  • Χ (Distance): maintain meta-position and reflective stop-capability; no fusion into verdict, urgency, enforcement, role, accusation, or person-level attribution.
  • Reversibility: all readings remain scene-bound, revisable, non-final, and configuration-specific; no global person labels or irreversible interpretive capture.
  • D (Dignity-in-Practice): no shaming, ranking, humiliation, diagnosis, maturity hierarchy, dignity hierarchy, or ontological person evaluation; critique targets enacted structures and cost handling only.

3.2 Explicit non-goals (overlay discipline)

  • no clinical, therapeutic, or forensic use;
  • no personality typing, motive theory, or inner-state claims;
  • no prescriptive “how-to” or coercive action demands;
  • no moral ranking of persons; no ontological dignity judgments;
  • no ideology-based attribution: regimes are defined by operator behavior only;
  • no automatic extension into scenes better captured by rival frameworks or genuine non-capture cases;
  • no conversion of asymmetry switch into authority;
  • no use of parity-to-exit as reciprocity;
  • no use of responsibility-to-authority as care, protection, or adult responsibility.
Key firewall description vs PMS-valid application

PMS-EDEN allows sharp descriptive mapping: operators, costs, drift signatures, switch handles, and regime configurations. The moment the mapping is used to bind, obligate, prescribe, enforce, rank, diagnose, or authorize control, the validity gate is active. Bypassing Χ, reversibility, or D makes the move formally invalid as PMS application—even if the descriptive analysis is accurate.

4.2 Master trace (Eden drift corridor)

Core corridor (operator-readable; non-moral)

Eden → Threshold → NRK → □comparison → PS → ASYMMETRY_SWITCH_AVAILABILITY → PFO/PERMANENTIZED_EXCEPTION → (PARITY_TO_EXIT | RESPONSIBILITY_TO_AUTHORITY) → residual_stabilization(Λ/Φ/Α) → (DEVALUATION | HUMILIATION) → RECIPROCITY_LOSS

Switch availability and residual stabilization (Λ/Φ/Α) are kept explicit rather than compressed away.

4.3 Paper-internal composites (non-operators)

NRK (Negative Responsibility Kernel)

Breach-type label: enactment occurs while awareness options are available, yet Σ/Ψ do not carry the enactment. NRK is a paper-internal composite descriptor, not a PMS operator.

PS (Pseudo-Symmetry)

Rhetorical equality inside a comparison-dominant □ under real Ω in consequences, with Σ blocked or simulated and Ψ misbound to appearance-management. In the updated profile, PS also prepares switch availability when parity remains the surface grammar while Ω becomes selectively activatable.

AS / Asymmetry switch

Paper-internal label for the shift from parity claim to asymmetry activation under real or invoked Ω/Θ pressure. Valid only where activation is marked, bounded, and re-bound into Σ/Ψ; drifting where unmarked, repeatable, and responsibility-shifting.

PES / Permanentized exception switch

PFO drift form in which asymmetry activation ceases to be emergency-bound and becomes a standing lever while parity remains rhetorically preserved and Σ/Ψ coordination remains blocked.

PTE / Parity-to-exit

Drift pattern in which parity or symmetry language is used not to coordinate reciprocity, but to deny additional responsibility under unequal Ω/Θ consequences.

RTA / Responsibility-to-authority

Drift pattern in which required self-binding under asymmetry is converted into control, protection entitlement, frame authority, or governance. Greater responsibility under asymmetry does not generate authority.

PFO (Postfeminist Override / Over-steering)

Paper-internal regime label for a regime-level meta-frame: □ is configured as comparison metric + Ω-taboo + permanentized exception-switch availability. Φ/Λ/Α stabilize while Σ is suppressed and Ψ is displaced or misbound. Defined only by operator behavior; not ideological; not a group label.

Humiliation / devaluation (structural use)

Paper-internal meaning: status regulation via devaluation as residual stabilization when explicit Ω-coordination via Σ/Ψ is blocked. This is a structural consequence claim, not an intent, motive, trait, or moral verdict.

Adult reciprocity principle

PMS-EDEN defines reciprocity as coordinated asymmetry, not symmetry: Σ under Ω, bound by Ψ across Θ, limited by Χ, constrained by D. Adult reciprocity does not require equal burdens; it requires non-externalization of unequal burdens.

Non-promissory stance

PMS-EDEN is descriptive and criterial: it makes drift, switch mechanics, and cost geometry inspectable. It does not offer optimization, therapy, instruction, or authority claims. Where “correction signatures” are mentioned, they are viability descriptions only and remain subject to the validity gate.

5. EDEN-MAP (method appendix): scene-bound mapping protocol

EDEN-MAP is a structural mapping protocol for PMS-EDEN analyses. It identifies operator configurations (Δ–Ψ) in a given scene without diagnosing persons. It is question-driven and includes a mandatory diagnostics-to-action firewall.

Cartographic status not a closed ontology

EDEN-MAP is not a closed ontology of relational breakdown. It is a cartographic compression of the structural patterns reconstructed in PMS-EDEN. Its purpose is to increase portability, comparability, and auditability, not to claim that every relevant case is already exhausted by its map-space.

The map remains revisable, scene-dependent in application, and open to rival framings and genuine non-capture. The more portable the map becomes, the greater the risk that compression is mistaken for structural completeness.

5.1 Minimal unit: Scene Packet (15.2)

SceneID: {Θwindow, □anchor, roles}
Trigger: {Δ, ∇} · Λ candidates: [...]
Realizations: {observed, omissions, escalations}
E-check: {E_present?, Σcarrier?, notes} (discipline: realization ≠ E without Σ-carriage)
Α stabilizers: [...] · Ω real: {capacity, exposure, obligation, leverage}
Ψ map: {declared, enacted, enforced, externalized} (incl. Ψ→Other demands)
Switch map: {asymmetry_activation, parity_to_exit, responsibility_to_authority, re_binding}

5.2 Core questions Q1–Q7 (15.3)

Q1 — Which frame (□) dominates?

Identify the dominant relevance grammar (□praxis, □comparison, □moral, □security, □scarcity, □narrative, or hybrid). Output: □dominant = <type/hybrid> + what is permitted, forbidden, speakable, or punishable.

Q2 — What asymmetry (Ω) is real, and how is it handled?

Map gradients: capacity, exposure, obligation, leverage. Record whether Ω is nameable/coordinable, taboo, selectively activated, weaponized, or unclear under □. Output: Ωreal = {capacity, exposure, obligation, leverage} + Ω-handling = <coordinated|taboo|selectively_activated|weaponized|unclear>.

Q3 — Which Λ is active and how is it handled?

Identify the active non-event (reply/repair/recognition/coordination/commitment missing) and closure mode (denial, minimization, moralization, displacement, pseudo-closure). Output: Λactive / Λhandling / Λremainder.

Q4 — Where is Χ missing or instrumentalized?

Identify where distance is absent or punished and where “distance” is used as control (erasing Λ, avoiding Σ while keeping leverage, protecting a switch surface). Output: Χabsent / Χinstrumental / Χrestoration.

Q5 — Where is Σ low, failed, suppressed, or simulated?

Record integration status and blockers (□/Ω/Λ/Θ). Output: Σstatus / Σblockers / Σminimum.

Q6 — Who self-binds (Ψ), and who externalizes binding (Ψ→Other)?

Map declared vs enacted vs enforced binding; identify Ψ→Other demands. Output: Ψself / Ψexternal / Ψrepair (repair = shift from other-control to self-binding under D).

Q7 — Does switch drift appear?

Test whether asymmetry activation is marked, bounded, and re-bound into Σ/Ψ, or whether drift appears as unmarked asymmetry activation, parity-to-exit, or responsibility-to-authority. Output: switch_drift = {asymmetry_activation, parity_to_exit, responsibility_to_authority, re_binding}.

5.3 One-page synthesis (15.4)

Required synthesis fields

Drift = □dominant → Λhandling → Αstabilizer → Ωmanagement/switch_drift → Σstatus → Ψpattern
Αprimary (what repetition stabilizes the configuration)
Costs = {A, C, R, E, D} (system costs; non-moral)
LowestCostCorrection = {Χstep, Λ→Σstep, switch_step, Ψshift} (descriptive viability signature; not a prescription)

Switch step in synthesis

switch_step means: mark, bound, and re-bind asymmetry activation; block parity-to-exit and responsibility-to-authority; preserve Χ, reversibility, and D. It is a viability signature, not an instruction or enforcement license.

Mandatory firewall (15.5)

The restrictions in this section apply to PMS-valid application, not to all possible discourse about persons, scenes, or institutions. EDEN-MAP is not a person-evaluation tool. It permits scene-bound structural mapping, drift-risk and cost geometry, scene-bound switch-drift handles, and reversible dignity-preserving viability signatures. It forbids global person labels, inner-state claims, clinical categories, coercive prescriptions justified by PMS vocabulary, or converting responsibility into authority over another role-position.

7. Drift catalogue (corridors, regimes, switch drift, reciprocity loss)

Drift markers are configuration statements (“in this scene, the following operators are carrying / failing to carry…”), not person labels. The catalogue consolidates predictable consequences of operator constellations under repetition and time.

7.1 Core drift corridor (compressed)

Eden → Threshold → NRK → □comparison → PS → ASYMMETRY_SWITCH_AVAILABILITY → PFO/PERMANENTIZED_EXCEPTION → (PARITY_TO_EXIT | RESPONSIBILITY_TO_AUTHORITY) → residual_stabilization(Λ/Φ/Α) → (DEVALUATION | HUMILIATION) → RECIPROCITY_LOSS

7.2 Regime-level repeatability (PFO as meta-frame)

PFO — paper-internal regime label; repeatability without ideology attribution

PFO denotes a dominant □ configured as Ω-illegibility / Ω-taboo + comparison metric + permanentized exception-switch availability, with high Φ (continuous recontextualization), persistent Λ (non-closure as remainder or leverage), and stabilized Α scripts. Σ and Ψ remain suppressed, displaced, or misbound, so reciprocity cannot consolidate. This is defined only by operator behavior, not political identity, ideology, group membership, or person type.

7.3 Switch drift branches

Branch Drift form Validity condition / failure point
ASYMMETRY_SWITCH Asymmetry is activated under real or invoked Ω/Θ pressure. Valid where marked, bounded, and re-bound into Σ/Ψ; drift where unmarked, repeatable, and responsibility-shifting.
PERMANENTIZED_EXCEPTION Exception-switch becomes standing regime availability under parity surface. Fails where asymmetry activation is no longer emergency-bound or coordinatively re-bound.
PARITY_TO_EXIT Parity or equality language exits responsibility under unequal Ω/Θ consequences. Fails where equality-language replaces cost re-internalization.
RESPONSIBILITY_TO_AUTHORITY Required Ψ/Χ responsibility is converted into authority, protection entitlement, control, or governance. Fails where responsibility stops being self-binding and becomes authorization.

7.4 Reciprocity loss (tragedy, not guilt)

Reciprocity = coordinated asymmetry (PMS-EDEN definition)

Reciprocity = Σ under Ω, bound by Ψ across Θ, limited by Χ. Loss of reciprocity is readable as collapse of these carriers under comparison-dominant □ plus chronic Λ across Θ. Under the switch extension, reciprocity also fails where parity becomes exit, asymmetry becomes leverage, or responsibility becomes authority.

Cost marker (non-moral)

Where mappings repeatedly show suppressed Ω-legibility, unmarked Ω activation without re-binding, low or simulated Σ, Ψ→Other externalization or Ψ misbinding, parity-to-exit, responsibility-to-authority, chronic Λ, and stabilized Α scripts, PMS-EDEN treats the configuration as a drift regime sustained by asymmetric cost carriage. This is a structural non-viability signal under PMS criteria as used in this paper, not a moral verdict.

9. Scope discipline: alternative explanations and boundary conditions

9.1 Alternative explanations (methodological discipline)

PMS-EDEN explicitly lists alternative sufficient generators to prevent over-claiming. No refutation is attempted; no ranking is implied. Alternatives are phrased in role/gradient language (Ω-capacity/exposure/leverage positions), not group labels. All AE use remains scene-bound and structurally revisable.

AE1 — Drift without regime

Pair-dynamics under stable □comparison can generate monitoring, pseudo-symmetry, chronic Λ, and Α scripts without a regime layer.

AE2 — Drift via Ω overextension

Drift can begin when the most Ω-salient position mis-handles leverage without Χ/D, triggering counter-control and frame shift.

AE3 — Drift without humiliation

Stabilization can occur via withdrawal, exit, or isolation rather than humiliation: Χ increases as withdrawal; Θ locks separation.

AE4 — External institutional stress

Drift can be driven by institutional scarcity, compliance, sanction logics, throughput pressure, or administrative overload rather than by a locally stabilized regime logic; Α then stabilizes procedural survival scripts.

9.2 Boundary conditions (where the pipeline weakens)

Boundary condition Effect on PMS-EDEN drift claims
Ω minimal or absent Asymmetry-driven drift mechanisms lose their primary generator; applicability becomes partial.
□ not comparison-based Praxis frame remains dominant; Σ-work remains accessible; pseudo-symmetry less likely to stabilize.
Σ/Ψ established early Upstream interruption: reciprocity can stabilize before chronic Λ accumulates under Θ.
External institutions stabilize roles Contracts, norms, or institutional interfaces may scaffold Σ/Ψ and constrain □; regime-like drift becomes less likely.
Switch activation marked, bounded, and re-bound Asymmetry activation may remain viable and dignity-preserving when it is explicitly tied to real Ω/Θ burden and re-bound into Σ/Ψ.
Rival frameworks retain explanatory force In some scenes, trauma, attachment, symbolic recognition, institutional asymmetry, theological anthropology, or phenomenological alienation may remain more discriminative than PMS-EDEN.
Genuine non-capture cases Where PMS-EDEN would smooth over distinctions better preserved elsewhere, the model should be narrowed, suspended, or treated as partial.
Strongest applicability condition Highest discriminative force occurs when Ω is action-relevant, □ becomes comparison-dominant, Σ stays low, Ψ is absent/misbound/externalized, Λ becomes chronic, Α stabilizes scripts, and switch drift is available or already active.
Non-normalization clause

Reduced applicability limits explanatory reach, not viability criteria. Persistent cost externalization remains structurally non-viable under PMS criteria as used in this paper regardless of genesis path.

10. Positioning within adjacent frameworks (appendix layer)

This section is repository-facing: it situates PMS-EDEN relative to adjacent theoretical programs, clarifies non-goals, reduces predictable misclassification (psychological, theological, ideological, normative), and preserves rival-framing openness without replacement or superiority claims.

Neighbor Overlap Non-equivalence / boundary value
Practice theory Anti-mentalism; situated enactment; structured conditions. More operator-minimal + explicit validity gate for application.
Structuration Structure ↔ practice coupling; reproduction and constraint. Explicit drift pivot (□ praxis → □ value-relation) + Ω/Θ/Λ consequence legibility.
Ethnomethodology Order production; accountability surfaces. More schematic/generative; designed for cross-scene comparability.
Systems theory Drift, stabilization, regime reproduction. Praxeological and role-position oriented (Ω gradients); explicit Dignity-in-Practice (D) as viability boundary without moralization.
Pragmatic sociology Value frames and justification grammars. Drift modeled as operator carriage failure under Ω/Θ/Λ; dissatisfaction framed as system maintenance cost, not deficit.
Theological contrast Contrast class only. Eden as minimal structural test scene, not doctrine or moral anthropology; tragedy defined structurally, not as desert.
Label risk control

If a regime label is predictably politicized or misread, the preferred fix is lower-entropy renaming while preserving the operator definition, paper-internal label discipline, and non-ideological stance.
