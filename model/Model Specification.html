1. Purpose and scope

PMS-EDEN is a paper-specific application schema for the Praxeological Meta-Structure. It is an overlay, not a fork, replacement, extension, or second operator system. It formalizes the paper's argument in a machine-readable and human-auditable way while remaining subordinate to the canonical PMS operator grammar.

The specification's positive starting point is Adult Reciprocity: truthful coordination of real Ω. Its central mechanism is False-Coordinated Real Asymmetry. Its culminating loss is Reciprocity Loss, defined as the erosion of shared correctability under real asymmetry.

Scope in one sentence

Tests whether real Ω is truthfully coordinated through R, Σ, Ψ, Χ, D, and correctability or falsely coordinated through displaced R and blocked Σ/Ψ.

5. Validity gate and Description-to-Action Firewall

The PMS entry condition applies to application, not to critique, refusal, rejection, revision proposals, or rival formalization. EDEN adds application requirements because its scenes are high-risk: they concern real Ω, responsibility, protection, recognition, cost, exposure, and correctability.

Canonical PMS entry condition

  • Χ: Reflective stop-capability; no fusion into urgency, verdict, enforcement, ideology, or person attribution.
  • reversibility: Reading remains scene-bound, revisable, narrowable, suspendable, and defeasible.
  • D: No shaming, ranking, humiliation, ontological evaluation, sex ranking, or degradation.

EDEN application requirements

  • scene_packet: Specified scene, role-positions, real Ω, effects, costs, Θ, and Σ/Ψ relevance.
  • counter_scene_exposure: A plausible materially different scene can change the reading.
  • contextual_responsibility: R attribution tracks effect under legibility, capacity, alternatives, Θ, and Ω cost/exposure.
  • evidentiary_answerability: No concept, pattern, frame, role code, or label replaces evidence.
  • affected_role_contestation: Affected roles may contest packet, evidence, frame, R trace, or application without confirming EDEN by disagreement.
  • independent_application_authority: EDEN-MAP supplies no authority to confront, accuse, prescribe, intervene, punish, or enforce.

Invalid if any

  • Χ_suspended_for_clarity_or_urgency
  • reversibility_suspended_for_verdict
  • D_suspended_for_truth_or_efficiency
  • scene_packet_replaced_by_impression
  • counter_scene_cannot_change_reading
  • responsibility_without_scene_conditions
  • description_converted_directly_into_binding
  • person_or_sex_label_substituted_for_scene_analysis

Description versus application

Description: Operator-traceable scene mapping without obligation.

Application: Mapping used to steer, bind, obligate, prescribe, confront, accuse, intervene, punish, or enforce.

Permitted descriptive outputs

  • name_real_asymmetry
  • trace_structuring_effect
  • identify_possible_responsibility_displacement
  • identify_possible_integration_or_self_binding_blockage
  • record_counter_scenes_and_falsifiers

Forbidden conversions from description

  • binding
  • obligation
  • confrontation
  • accusation
  • prescription
  • intervention
  • punishment
  • enforcement

Core firewall formulas

  • Description may name structure.
  • Application requires Χ, reversibility, D, and contextual responsibility.
  • Description does not authorize binding.

6. Core thesis and architecture invariants

Adult Reciprocity is truthful coordination of real Ω; Reciprocity Loss begins where real Ω is falsely coordinated, R displaced, Σ/Ψ blocked, and shared correctability eroded.

Positive baseline

  • Adult Reciprocity ≠ symmetry.
  • Adult Reciprocity = truthful coordination of real Ω.

Explicit EDEN benchmark; not total access, metaphysical truth, empirical universal, or automatic application.

Negative counterpart

  • Reciprocity Loss = false coordination of real Ω.
  • Reciprocity Loss is the erosion of shared correctability under real asymmetry.

Governing distinction

  • Asymmetry is not the failure.
  • False coordination of asymmetry is the failure.

Master trace

coordinated Ω → false-coordinated real Ω → displaced R → blocked Σ/Ψ → reciprocity loss

Structural trace; not chronology, deterministic cause, PMS dependency, or universal sequence.

Central mechanism preview

real Ω + false coordination frame + displaced R + blocked Σ/Ψ = false-coordinated real Ω

  • The asymmetry need not be false; the coordination is false.
  • Partial framing alone is insufficient.
  • Scene evidence must establish effect, displaced R, and Σ/Ψ relevance.

8. Adult Reciprocity and positive baseline

Truthful coordination of real Ω; 'Adult' stipulates structural answerability, not age, psychological maturity, moral superiority, advice, or typology.

real Ω visible + D preserved + R follows real structuring effect + Σ integrates costs and effects + Ψ binds relevant actors to their own effect + Χ keeps revision possible

Adult Reciprocity is not equivalent to

  • symmetry
  • sameness
  • harmony
  • equal_burden
  • formal_equality
  • conflict_absence
  • abolition_of_asymmetry

Failure threshold

Where real Ω structures the scene but D cannot be preserved, R cannot follow real structuring effect, Σ cannot integrate costs and effects, Ψ cannot bind role-positions to their own effects, and Χ cannot keep revision possible, Adult Reciprocity begins to fail.

Real Ω remains operative but a frame displaces R, blocks Σ, weakens/externalizes Ψ, and erodes Adult Reciprocity/correctability; asymmetry need not be false, coordination is false.

10. Responsibility and praxis architecture

No Passive Praxis

Visible action does not exhaust scene-structuring praxis. Non-action becomes praxis-relevant only as a meaningful framed non-event affecting real asymmetry across time and traceably changing another role-position's action conditions, costs, exposure, R, integration, self-binding, or correctability.

  • No Passive Praxis ≠ every non-action is action.
  • Non-action becomes praxis only when it structures a scene under □/Λ/Ω/Θ.
  • Praxis ≠ strict Action E.
  • Strict Action / Enactment E = Σ ∘ Θ ∘ ∇.

The stop criterion is strict: no traceable change in another role-position's options, costs, exposure, timing, binding load, integration, or correctability means no No Passive Praxis reading.

LevelMeaningBoundary
Mere absenceNothing relevant happens, or no scene-structuring effect is traceable.No praxis finding by absence alone.
Structuring non-eventA framed non-event changes options, costs, timing, exposure, R, Σ, Ψ, or correctability under real Ω.Scene effect must be shown; no motive follows.
Integrated abstentionNon-action is bounded, legible, responsibility-compatible, and integrated into Σ/Ψ.Restraint, distance, incapacity, boundary, and legitimate non-involvement remain possible.

Responsibility Principle

R follows real structuring effect under available legibility, capacity, alternative possibility, Θ trajectory, and Ω cost/exposure conditions.

This principle connects answerability to what a role-position materially structures in options, timing, access, exposure, protection, recognition, language, cost, criticizability, integration, binding, or correction.

  • R is not blame.
  • R is not guilt.
  • R is not moral worth.
  • R is not person condemnation.
  • R is not mere causality.
  • R is not outcome-liability alone.
  • Responsibility may remain real without malice.
  • Responsibility remains limited by legibility, capacity, alternatives, Θ, and Ω.

Responsibility and Action-Power

  • Visible action is one responsibility vector.
  • Structuring non-action is another responsibility vector.
  • Action may create responsibility, but responsibility is not identical with action.
  • Non-action may create responsibility-relevant effects, but non-action is not automatically responsibility.

Relevant vectors include direct action, indirect Action-Power, framing, timing control, withholding, recognition allocation, protection allocation, access control, cost distribution, legitimation, non-binding, and structuring non-action. Each vector must separately pass real effect, legibility, capacity, alternatives, temporality, and asymmetrical-cost tests.

Non-binding as Ψ-load shift

  • low Ψ on one side → increased Ψ-load on the other.
  • I do not bind myself, but you must organize yourself around my non-binding.

Possible shifted loads include waiting, option preservation, timing management, interpretation, explanation, continuity, self-regulation, critique restraint, and repair. Naming a Ψ-load shift does not authorize compelled clarity, commitment, reassurance, availability, disclosure, confession, or continued engagement.

Immunization / Overexposure / AEP

  • Immunization = effect without full responsibility.
  • Overexposure = responsibility without full legibility / protection.
  • AEP = high exposure + low legitimate language + critique-to-threat switch + responsibility overload.

AEP does not mean stupidity, incapacity, moral inferiority, lack of articulation, sex defect, innocence, or cancellation of R. It must be tested under legibility, capacity, alternatives, Θ, Ω, and counter-scene exposure.

Profiles remain scene-bound drift patterns, not sex truths. Naming overexposure does not immunize the overexposed role-position from bounded R; naming immunization does not authorize degradation of the immunized role-position.

Recognition Drift

Recognition, dignity, voice, protection, moral standing, or equal status detaches from criticizability, consequence attribution, self-binding, correction, and R for real structuring effect.

  • Recognition without answerability is not equal standing.
  • Equal standing = recognition + answerability + criticizability + consequence attribution + correctability.
  • Dignity is not immunity.
  • Responsibility is not degradation.
  • Protection from degradation ≠ protection from responsibility.
  • Structure critique ≠ person threat.

11. PFO, Eden scene, and Reciprocity Loss

PFO

One downstream regime form where false-coordinated real asymmetry, displaced R, blocked Σ/Ψ, and established drift mechanisms stabilize through repeatable recognition, protection, parity, or exception frames.

  • PFO is not the origin of reciprocity loss.
  • PFO is one regime form in which reciprocity loss stabilizes.
  • PFO is downstream regime stabilization.
  • PFO = regime stabilization of false-coordinated real Ω under recognition / protection / parity / exception frames with displaced R and blocked Σ/Ψ.
  • PFO is repeatable frame availability under real Ω.

Eden scene

Late compact demonstration of existing grammar: coordinated asymmetry, meaningful boundary, breach, consequence, exposure, legibility management, and R trace without theoretical, moral, theological, sexed, or historical origin claim.

  • Eden is not the paper's origin.
  • Eden is a test scene for the paper's grammar.
  • Eden is not foundation.
  • Eden is demonstration.
  • Eden supports the paper's late-test-scene discipline.
  • It does not replace the architecture.

Reciprocity Loss

Structural loss where real Ω persists while R is displaced, Σ blocked, Ψ weakened/externalized, Χ reduced, D damaged, and shared capacity for proportionate correction erodes or disappears.

  • Reciprocity Loss = false coordination of real Ω.
  • Reciprocity Loss is the erosion of shared correctability under real asymmetry.
  • Reciprocity loss is not merely conflict.
  • Conflict can remain compatible with reciprocity.
  • Reciprocity loss begins when correction itself becomes illegible, unsafe, one-sided, humiliating, immunized, or impossible.
  • Asymmetry is not the failure.
  • False coordination of asymmetry is the failure.

Falsifier categories

  • Ω is minimal or absent.
  • The alleged non-action does not structure costs or options.
  • Responsibility remains properly attached to effect.
  • Critique remains possible.
  • Σ/Ψ are robustly open.
  • Protection is bounded and responsibility-compatible.
  • Vulnerability remains criticizable without degradation.
  • Hierarchy is bounded and functional.
  • A rival explanation is stronger.
  • The alleged pattern is not repeated or consequential.
  • A counter-scene explains the scene better.

Counter-scene protocol

Every strong EDEN reading must test at least one counter-scene where the same facts do not imply false-coordinated real Ω.

  • A counter-scene must be able to change the reading.
  • Otherwise it is decorative, not methodological.
  • Counter-scene is not politeness.
  • Counter-scene is a methodological exposure to non-capture.

13. EDEN-MAP

EDEN-MAP orders scene-bound questions to test support, uncertainty, narrowing, secondary status, or defeat of false-coordinated real Ω, displaced R, blocked Σ/Ψ, and drift mechanisms. It adds no operator, mechanism, diagnosis, verdict, or authority.

  • EDEN-MAP maps scenes.
  • It maps what may be structurally happening.
  • It does not tell what should be done.
  • It does not diagnose persons.
  • It does not rank sexes.
  • It does not authorize coercive action.
  • It does not replace counter-scene testing.

Input limits

  • No isolated-sentence diagnosis.
  • No identity-based application.
  • No EDEN-by-vibe.
  • No application by label.
  • No application by impression.
  • No theory label in place of a scene.
  • No frame-only reading without effects and costs.
  • No actor-only reading without asymmetry and temporality.
