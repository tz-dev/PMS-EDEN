# PMS–EDEN

## Structural Drift from Praxis to Comparison, Pseudo-Symmetry, and Reciprocity Loss

**PMS–EDEN** is a domain-specific application overlay of the **Praxeological Meta-Structure Theory (PMS)**. It applies the canonical PMS operator grammar (Δ–Ψ) to the structural problem of **drift from praxis to comparison**: how a praxis-ordered configuration collapses into comparison, how asymmetry becomes status-legible, how pseudo-symmetry stabilizes drift, and why reciprocity can erode under time, residue, and failed coordination.

PMS–EDEN does **not** modify, extend, reinterpret, or replace the PMS operator set. It is an operator-strict overlay that uses PMS as its structural foundation.

Its central thesis is:

> Eden is not lost through insight, knowledge, or moral awakening.  
> Eden is lost when praxis coordination fails under available awareness and consequence-bearing asymmetry.

In this sense, PMS–EDEN reconstructs Eden as a praxeological drift trace:

```text
praxis-ordered configuration
→ threshold / option visibility
→ breach under available awareness
→ comparison-dominant frame
→ pseudo-symmetry
→ residual stabilization
→ reciprocity loss
```

## 4. Core Thesis

PMS–EDEN argues that:

- the decisive shift is not “knowledge,” but a praxis breach under available awareness;
- the relevant awareness structure can be represented as:

```text
A = [Θ, □, Δ]
```

- post-breach, Ω becomes consequence-legible under Θ;
- □ tends to drift into a value-relation, comparison frame, or ranking surface;
- structurally different configurations (Δ) treated as equivalent inside a comparison frame generate a compulsion loop:
  - permanent comparison,
  - permanent correction,
  - permanent dissatisfaction;
- dissatisfaction becomes a system cost, not necessarily evidence of objective lack;
- pseudo-symmetry stabilizes drift by substituting Φ / Λ / Α for Σ / Ψ under real Ω;
- reciprocity becomes structurally compromised when coordinated asymmetry cannot consolidate.

The compact formulation is:

> Eden is not lost by insight.  
> It is lost when coordination (Σ) and binding (Ψ) fail under legible consequence trajectories (Θ).

## 10. Discriminative Adequacy and Non-Capture

The strength of a PMS–EDEN reconstruction is not measured only by how much material it can translate into operator terms.

It is measured by whether that translation **preserves or increases differentiating force** relative to rival framings.

A reconstruction becomes methodologically weaker where it:

- absorbs divergent material too smoothly,
- flattens distinctions that rival models keep explanatorially sharp,
- treats all resistance as already internal to its own grammar,
- or converts non-capture into proof of applicability.

PMS–EDEN therefore leaves open the possibility of **genuine non-capture**.

Some cases may:

- resist its grammar,
- apply only partially,
- be better described by rival frameworks,
- or require empirical, historical, theological, political, or psychological analysis outside PMS.

Showing that a configuration is operatorically coherent does **not** by itself show that it is:

- empirically dominant,
- historically typical,
- explanatorily primary,
- universally valid,
- or morally authoritative.

PMS–EDEN distinguishes between:

```text
structural legibility
recurrent drift tendency
empirical prevalence
```

### 11.2 Threshold and Breach Discipline

The threshold marker does not add knowledge.

The breach does not create epistemic gain.

Within PMS–EDEN, breach yields:

- consequence-legibility,
- irreversibility,
- residue,

- and new drift corridors.

### 11.3 NRK as Paper-Internal Composite

PMS–EDEN introduces **NRK (Negative Responsibility Kernel)** as a compact paper-internal composite for:

- enactment under available awareness,
- failed Σ-carriage,
- failed or displaced Ψ,
- effective Ω,
- and trajectory-bearing Θ.

NRK is not a PMS operator.

It is a scene-bound structural composite.

### 11.4 False Comparison and Dissatisfaction

PMS–EDEN formalizes how a praxis frame can drift into comparison-dominant □:

```text
Δ becomes rank-token
Ω becomes verdict-surface
Σ / Ψ coordination carriers are suppressed
dissatisfaction becomes system-maintenance cost
```

Dissatisfaction is therefore not automatically evidence of objective lack.

It may indicate the cost of maintaining false comparison.

### 11.5 Pseudo-Symmetry as Substitution Regime

Pseudo-Symmetry (PS) is reconstructed as:

- rhetorical parity under real Ω,
- low or simulated Σ,
- Ψ misbound to appearance-management,
- Φ / Λ / Α acting as substitute stabilizers.

PS can stabilize a field short-term while dissolving reciprocity long-term.

### 11.6 Reciprocity as Coordinated Asymmetry

A central PMS–EDEN thesis is:

> Reciprocity is not symmetry.  
> Reciprocity is coordinated asymmetry.

Reciprocity requires:

- Ω to remain nameable as functional gradient,
- Σ to coordinate real burdens and interfaces,
- Ψ to remain self-binding rather than outwardly imposed,
- Χ to limit escalation and preserve reversibility,
- Θ to carry continuity without residue collapse.

### 11.7 Pattern Library and Drift Recognition

The overlay organizes a pattern library of paper-internal drift composites such as:

- Ω → covert authority,
- Λ → denial / suppression,
- Ψ → externalization,
- Σ-simulation,
- moral frame capture,
- comparison compulsion,
- reciprocity erosion.

These are **scene-bound recognition aids**, not diagnoses and not enforcement licenses.

### 11.8 EDEN-MAP

PMS–EDEN includes **EDEN-MAP**, a structural mapping protocol for bounded scenes.

EDEN-MAP:

- maps operator constellations (Δ–Ψ),
- keeps roles, frames, non-events, and cost geometry explicit,
- separates description from application,
- enforces Χ + reversibility + D,
- forbids person-evaluation, diagnosis, and coercive prescription.

`model/PMS-EDEN.yaml` is the current repository reference overlay specification for the PMS–EDEN application layer.

This means:

- it records overlay-level constructs, master trace, EDEN-MAP protocol, pattern library, application guardrails, and example-suite requirements used across the current repository version;
- it coordinates human-readable artefacts, examples, and model specifications within this repository;
- it does not imply that Eden, reciprocity, gendered asymmetry, comparison drift, or relational breakdown are exhausted by PMS treatment;
- it does not imply that rival framings are invalid;
- it does not imply that all scenes must translate cleanly into PMS chains.

The overlay explicitly includes:

```text
methodological restraint
non-capture openness
scene-bound analysis
claim-typed application
discriminative adequacy
```

## 30. Final Note

PMS–EDEN does not tell you who is guilty.

It explains how drift becomes structurally stable when:

- comparison captures the frame (□),
- asymmetry becomes status-legible (Ω),
- residues stay open (Λ),
- scripts repeat (Α),
- recontextualization substitutes for integration (Φ),
- and coordination / binding (Σ / Ψ) are replaced by substitutes.

It also insists that this legibility remains valid only where the model stays:

- scene-bound,
- revisable,
- non-moralizing,
- non-diagnostic,
- non-coercive,
- and accountable to counter-scenes and rival framings.

PMS–EDEN becomes structurally invalid when it:

- turns drift labels into person labels,
- turns comparison analysis into moral ranking,
- turns pseudo-symmetry into accusation,
- turns asymmetry into gender essence,
- turns Eden into theology replacement,
- turns legibility into verdict,
- or turns structural mapping into coercive prescription.

> Eden is not repaired by verdicts.  
> It is repaired, if at all, by reopening coordination under real asymmetry.
