Minimal Vignette

Scene
After a praxis interruption in a minimal Eden-style scene, two role-positions stand in a consequence field where bodily exposure has become newly consequential. The body is not newly known; it is newly framed as exposed under Ω. Role A gathers leaves and uses them to cover exposed bodily visibility. Role B is present within the same altered exposure field, but the scene packet does not establish accusation, seduction, shame, guilt, deception, or a settled interpersonal regime. The analytic question is whether the covering gesture can be read as legibility management under newly exposed Ω without converting it into psychology.
Temporal Boundary
From consequence activation after the praxis interruption to the immediate covering gesture; later blame, punishment, theology, history, or regime stabilization are outside the scene.
Surface Description
The scene may initially appear as shame, guilt, or psychological embarrassment.
EDEN Question
Can the fig-leaf gesture be mapped as minimal legibility management under newly exposed Ω without diagnosing inner states or moral guilt?
