Short Scene

A breach has already occurred and the field has changed irreversibly. What becomes newly salient is not insight but exposure: some role-positions now stand in a more visible and vulnerable relation to consequence, while others can observe, interpret, or leverage that visibility. The first operative moves are not integrative or explanatory. Instead, visibility is reduced through covering, shielding, boundary-setting, and other forms of exposure attenuation. These moves repeat because they locally work, and repetition begins to stabilize into scripts before any later narrative interpretation appears. Later drift may convert shielding into opacity, leverage, or frame-control, but the initial fig-leaf move remains legibility management rather than psychology.

Structural Focus

The case examines PMS-EDEN's fig-leaf logic as legibility management without psychologizing. The decisive issue is how post-breach consequence legibility under Ω/Θ/Λ shifts □ toward vulnerability management, activates reactive Χ as shielding, and seeds Α as repeatable protective script. The updated PMS-EDEN reading preserves the distinction between immediate reactive shielding and later drift forms such as asymmetry-switch availability, parity-to-exit, responsibility-to-authority, or PFO.

Readable Structural Closure

This case shows that the fig-leaf move in PMS-EDEN is not a psychology of shame but an operator-level response to newly legible exposure. Once Ω becomes visible and Θ has already altered the scene, shielding is often the first viable move because reducing visibility is cheaper than restoring integration. What stabilizes first is not insight, but protective inhibition and the early formation of repeatable scripts. The updated reading adds that later opacity, switch drift, or authority-conversion must not be projected backward into the original shielding move. PMS-EDEN therefore reads fig-leaf dynamics as legibility management under asymmetry, not as interior confession or reflective achievement.
