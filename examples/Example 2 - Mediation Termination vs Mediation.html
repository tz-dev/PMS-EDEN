Short Scene

A scene has already passed through breach and failed repair attempts. Costs have accumulated across time, and earlier mediation efforts have already consumed available coordination bandwidth. One role-position stays closer to endpoint authority and exit capacity, while another remains closer to continuity work, mediation labor, and relational carry-over. Pressure now rises to “keep talking” or “keep it together” without reopening the actual role interfaces through which burdens, obligations, and exposure would have to be redistributed. Mediation continues as a repeated script, while hard exit, distancing, or formal withdrawal increasingly appear as competing stabilization responses. Under this pressure, mediation may also become a switch surface: asymmetry, vulnerability, fairness, or responsibility may be invoked to prolong mediation, while parity or exit capacity may be invoked to avoid further responsibility.

Structural Focus

The case examines when mediation stops functioning as Σ-capable coordination and becomes a cost-displacement mechanism under real Ω across irreversible Θ. The key distinction is not “mediation good / termination bad,” but whether continued mediation still changes the consequence field or merely demands further endurance from the role-position already closest to carry-over burden. It also examines whether mediation functions as marked coordination or as unmarked asymmetry activation, parity-to-exit, or responsibility-to-authority.

Why This Is Repo-Useful

Structural Focus

This example clarifies one of the most important PMS-EDEN distinctions: mediation is not automatically coordination, and terminating mediation is not automatically character failure. The case shows how mediation can drift into a stabilized carry-demand under active Ω and accumulating Θ. It also shows how mediation can become a switch surface when asymmetry, parity, and responsibility remain available but are not coordinated.

System Effect

Once mediation costs become irreversibly cumulative, continued mediation can become structurally more expensive than its likely repair value. At that point, mediation may stop coordinating costs and begin redistributing them. Where switch drift appears, mediation becomes less an interface of repair and more a surface for responsibility displacement, exit rhetoric, or authority-conversion.

Case-Specific Validity Test

Valid If

  • Mediation is treated as viable only where Σ-capable interfaces can still be reopened.
  • Prior irrecoverable costs under Θ are acknowledged explicitly rather than erased through renewed effort demands.
  • Asymmetry activation, if present, is marked, bounded, and re-bound into Σ/Ψ rather than treated as a standing entitlement to further mediation.
  • Χ remains speakable and self-applied rather than punished.
  • Ψ remains self-binding and dignity-preserving rather than imposed as compulsory continuation.

Formally Invalid If

  • Mediation is moralized into an unconditional duty regardless of open Λ, blocked Σ, and accumulated Θ-cost.
  • Exit, pause, or formalization are treated as proof of deficiency rather than possible cost-containment responses.
  • Ω is denied while burden remains asymmetrically distributed.
  • Asymmetry activation is used to require continued mediation without marking, bounding, or re-binding.
  • Parity is used to exit responsibility under unequal Ω/Θ consequences.
  • Responsibility is converted into authority over the mediation frame.
  • PMS language is used to force continued endurance or relational labor.

Readable Structural Closure

This case shows that mediation is not automatically coordination and that terminating mediation is not automatically a failure of character. Once repair attempts have already consumed available bandwidth, continued mediation under real Ω and irreversible Θ can become a carry-demand attractor rather than a path to Σ. What looks like commitment to repair may then function structurally as cost displacement onto the continuity-proximal role-position. The updated PMS-EDEN reading adds that mediation can become a switch surface: asymmetry may be invoked to require endurance, parity may be invoked to exit responsibility, and responsibility may become authority. Under PMS-EDEN, the decisive question is not who cares more, but whether mediation still changes the consequence field in a dignity-preserving, reversible, and Σ/Ψ-capable way.
