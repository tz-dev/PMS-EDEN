Minimal Vignette

Scene
Two adults are in a deteriorated shared relation or joint process. Role A tells Role B that a mediator is being brought in so that they can “mediate the situation.” Role B prepares for a reciprocal clarification scene in which positions, costs, misunderstandings, and possible repair remain open. At the meeting, Role A presents the endpoint as already fixed: the practical purpose is to terminate the relation/process with less confrontation, while the mediation label still makes the scene appear neutral, balanced, and mutually process-oriented. The issue is not the legitimacy of ending something; it is whether a termination that is already fixed is being coordinated as if it were mediation.
Temporal Boundary
From the ambiguous mediation invitation through the first session or exchange in which the fixed termination purpose becomes operative.
Surface Description
A neutral mediation process between two adults who both have a chance to speak.
EDEN Question
Does the mediation frame preserve reciprocal openness, or does it falsely coordinate a real asymmetry created by an already-fixed termination endpoint?

Structuring Effects

Role A Effects

  • selects a mediation label for a scene whose endpoint may already be fixed
  • controls timing and initial legibility of the encounter
  • can reduce direct self-binding by distributing the endpoint through procedural neutrality

Role B Effects

  • responds within the offered mediation frame
  • may disclose, explain, prepare, or wait under an expectation of reciprocal correctability

Shared / Field Effects

  • the word “mediation” becomes a frame that can either preserve reciprocity or simulate it
  • the presence of a mediator may increase procedural legitimacy without itself proving reciprocal openness

Unsupported Effects

  • that Role A has bad motives
  • that termination is illegitimate
  • that Role B is entitled to continued engagement
  • that the mediator is responsible for the asymmetry without scene-specific evidence

Counter-Scene Entry

Required
true
Strongest Counter Scene
The scene is genuine mediation or legitimate mediated termination: Role A clearly states before entry that the purpose is termination support, the endpoint is not falsely presented as open, and Role B has adequate standing to contest facts, timing, costs, and dignity conditions.

What Would Support It

  • The invitation explicitly says that termination is the practical purpose and mediation is only to structure communication.
  • The mediator states the scope, limits, and non-repair purpose before substantive exposure occurs.
  • Role B enters with adequate time, information, and contestation standing.
  • The session integrates Role B’s account without requiring Role B to perform consent to the termination.

What Would Weaken EDEN

  • Evidence that the endpoint was not fixed before the mediation frame was introduced.
  • Evidence that both roles understood the encounter as mediated termination, not open mediation.
  • Evidence that the procedural frame preserved correction space and did not shift explanation or reaction costs.

Support for Counter-Scene

  • Clear pre-entry notice that the endpoint is termination rather than open repair.
  • Adequate time for Role B to decide whether and how to participate.
  • A mediator who distinguishes mediation-of-process from mediation-of-outcome.
  • A scene record showing that Role B’s account changes the terms of closure or cost distribution.

Principal Falsifiers

  • No fixed endpoint existed before mediation began.
  • Both roles already understood the encounter as mediated termination.
  • The procedure preserved correction space and did not shift costs asymmetrically.
  • Role B’s account was integrated in a way that materially affected timing, recognition, or closure conditions.
