1.1 Title

Equality rhetoric under irreversible cost gradients.

1.2 Scene boundaries

  • Out of scope:

1.3 Short scene

Two role-positions remain engaged across an extended coordination sequence. One role-position stays close to decision endpoints and closure authority, while the other remains close to continuity work, repair effort, and fallout handling. As unequal consequence exposure becomes increasingly legible, the scene does not reopen explicit coordination around real Ω. Instead, the frame drifts from praxis-coordination toward comparison and equivalence talk. Equality rhetoric rises, comparison cycles stabilize, and durable interface change fails to form.

1.4 Structural focus

The case examines how pseudo-symmetry (PS) stabilizes when real asymmetry in cost and exposure remains operative but becomes difficult or illegitimate to name within the dominant frame. The key issue is not whether equality is normatively desirable, but whether equivalence-talk substitutes for coordination under real Ω across Θ.

8) Validity Gate Reminder

8.1 PMS entry condition

  • Χ (Distance): Distance must remain available as reflective restraint. Requests for pause, revision, or non-fusion must not be converted into evidence of unfairness or guilt.
  • Reversibility: Any mapping must remain scene-bound and revisable. The analysis must not harden into global labels about persons or roles.
  • D (Dignity-in-Practice): Real Ω may be named structurally, but asymmetry handling must remain dignity-constrained: no shaming, ranking, humiliation, or coercive use of PMS vocabulary.

8.2 Case-specific validity test

Valid if:

  • Real asymmetry in consequences can be named without collapsing into blame or person-ranking.
  • Equality language does not substitute for coordination around burdens, obligations, and exposure.
  • Ψ remains primarily self-binding rather than a demand directed outward.
  • Χ remains speakable and protected rather than punished as avoidance.

Formally invalid if:

  • The analysis is used to force compliance or proof of fairness from the other role-position.
  • Ω is invoked as accusation rather than coordinated as structural gradient.
  • Χ is overridden through urgency, equivalence rhetoric, or moralized commitment language.
  • The case is converted into a diagnosis of character, motive, or worth.

10) Extended Structural Closure

10.1

The case begins with a coordination scene in which asymmetrical burdens become increasingly legible in consequences. One role-position stays close to visible endpoint decisions and closure authority, while another remains exposed to continuity work, repair load, and fallout handling across time. This does not yet require moral language. It already establishes a structural difference in what each role-position carries under real Ω across Θ.

10.2

The decisive drift occurs when this asymmetry is not reopened as a coordination problem, but reprocessed as a fairness and equivalence problem. The frame □ shifts from praxis toward comparison. Once that shift occurs, what should have remained an interface question—who carries which burden, under which conditions, with which reversibility—becomes a question of parity display, balance rhetoric, and justification cycles.

10.3

In this drifted frame, pseudo-symmetry (PS) stabilizes as rhetorical parity under real consequence asymmetry. The configuration no longer negotiates actual burdens. Instead, it performs equivalence. This is why the example is so central to PMS-EDEN: it shows how PS functions not as reciprocity, but as a substitute regime when real Ω-coordination is blocked or made illegitimate.

10.4

The non-event Λ remains decisive throughout. What is missing is not communication in general, but explicit renegotiation of obligations, burdens, and interfaces. Missing repair and missing interface revision continue to structure the scene. Because these remain absent, comparison and fairness rhetoric cannot count as resolution. They only produce a more coherent appearance around a still-open remainder.

10.5

Attractor formation Α enters when these comparison and justification loops repeat because they work locally. They reduce immediate tension without reopening the harder coordination problem. Over time, the repeated script becomes cheaper than explicit renegotiation. What appears as fairness maintenance can therefore stabilize into a cost-masking attractor in which durable load is redistributed into less visible continuity work.

10.6

The temporal dimension Θ is crucial. Endpoint decisions and repeated maintenance work do not carry the same time-form. One closure event can be absorbed as a visible cost; repeated repair and fallout handling compound more quietly. This temporal asymmetry is one reason the configuration can look symmetrical rhetorically while remaining asymmetrical structurally. Reciprocity weakens not because one side is morally worse, but because time distributes burdens differently than the dominant frame admits.

10.7

Distance Χ is endangered in a very specific way here. Requests for pause, revision, or non-fusion are no longer treated as reflective restraint. Instead, they are recoded as avoidance, unfairness, or lack of commitment. Once that happens, the frame not only suppresses asymmetry-speech; it also begins to punish the very conditions required for a PMS-valid reopening of the scene.

10.8

Self-binding Ψ is likewise distorted. Rather than binding each role-position to explicit coordination, burden clarity, and reversible handling of asymmetry, Ψ drifts outward. One role-position pressures the other to prove fairness, equality, or renewed compliance. This is why the example connects directly to PMS-EDEN's externalization logic: self-binding no longer stabilizes reciprocity; it becomes a pressure surface.

10.9

Integration Σ correspondingly remains low or simulated. The scene may speak in the language of equality, repair, or shared responsibility, yet the actual interfaces do not change. Burden distribution, decision authority, continuity exposure, and reversibility conditions remain uncoordinated. In this sense, the configuration exemplifies Σ-simulation rather than integration.

10.10

The structural result is clear: equality rhetoric under these conditions does not remove asymmetry. It masks and redistributes it. What the scene calls fairness may function as a durable cost-shift into less visible continuity work. PMS-EDEN therefore treats the configuration as a drift toward reciprocity loss, not because equality is normatively bad, but because rhetorical parity has replaced coordination under real Ω across Θ.
