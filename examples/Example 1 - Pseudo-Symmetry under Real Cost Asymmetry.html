Minimal Vignette

Scene

Two adult collaborators describe a joint deliverable as an equal partnership: both are said to be equally free to adjust, pause, or decide later. In the bounded scene, role_A repeatedly withholds final confirmation until late in the deadline window while retaining approval over whether the deliverable can proceed. Role_B keeps the file open, absorbs third-party inquiries, preserves availability, and explains the delay. The operative language remains mutual flexibility and equal ownership, but the waiting, explanation, option-loss, and external visibility costs concentrate on role_B.

Temporal Boundary

From the first missed confirmation window to the deadline handoff where role_B must either submit incompletely, wait further, or carry the delay outward.

Surface Description

Two equal collaborators are both exercising mutual flexibility under an open deadline process.

EDEN Question

Does the parity frame truthfully coordinate real Ω, or does formal symmetry conceal unequal cost, displaced R, and weakened Σ/Ψ?

Readable Structural Closure

Summary

This example maps a pseudo-symmetry configuration: the scene speaks equality, mutual flexibility, and shared responsibility while real Ω remains unevenly distributed through timing control, deadline exposure, waiting, explanation, and option-loss. The EDEN reading is retained as a bounded description because the parity frame appears to simulate Σ, displace R, and shift Ψ-load, but it remains revisable through the named counter-scene and falsifiers. It does not by itself establish PFO, Reciprocity Loss, motive, manipulation, or any stable person profile.
